## 1. Make the pricing differentiation explicit (priority)

Right now "unlimited generations" is just one item in a 6-item feature grid. Add a short line directly under the price ($20.91/month) that calls out the actual competitive advantage — most alternatives cap free/low-tier usage (e.g. 5 posts/day) or start much higher ($49+/mo) with credit-based limits.

Suggested line under the price:
> No credits. No daily caps. No per-post pricing. One price, everything included.

## 2. Merge "Brand voice" and "Writing samples" into a single card (priority)

These two cards currently say close to the same thing. Combine into one card with a sharper pitch: the value isn't just "it sounds like you," it's that the user never has to re-explain their voice every time, unlike prompting a general AI tool from scratch each session.

Suggested card copy:
> **Your voice, set once**
> Give it 3 writing samples one time. Every post after that — for as long as you use it — sounds like you, without re-explaining your tone every session.

Remove the two separate existing cards ("Brand voice" and "Writing samples") and replace with this single card. Use the freed-up grid slot for item #4 below.

## 3. Update the YouTube feature copy to reflect what it actually does (priority)

Currently YouTube is listed alongside LinkedIn, X, and Instagram as if it's the same kind of output (a post). It's not — it's title, description, and chapter generation for a video *before* the user publishes it, generated from an uploaded video file (audio is extracted and transcribed, then run through the same voice engine as the rest of the app).

This needs to be accurate so it doesn't read like a video-editing/clip tool (which it isn't) or get compared to that category.

Update anywhere YouTube is mentioned ("All major platforms" card, "Pick your platforms" step, pricing feature list) to be specific, e.g.:

> LinkedIn, X, and Instagram posts — plus YouTube title, description, and chapters generated from your video before you publish it.

Consider adding a fourth step or a short line in the "How it works" section acknowledging this is a slightly different flow (upload video vs. paste notes) rather than implying every platform works identically.

## 4. Downgrade "Live streaming output" from a top-level feature card

This is a nice UX touch but not something people choose the product for. Remove it as a standalone card in the "Everything you need" grid. Instead, mention it briefly as a detail in the "Get ready-to-post content" step copy (step 3 of "How it works"), e.g. appending something like "...streamed live to your screen as it's written" to the existing step 3 description, which already gestures at this.

Use the freed-up card slot (combined with the slot freed by item #2) for a card that reflects an actual differentiator — for example, something about the product being built for messy/unstructured input specifically (rough notes, half-formed ideas) rather than requiring a polished draft to start from, since that's a real gap versus competitors who assume clean input.

Suggested new card:
> **Built for messy ideas**
> A half-formed thought, a voice memo transcript, a bullet list at 11pm — you don't need a polished draft to start.

Competitors like PostEverywhere also support Threads, TikTok captions, and Pinterest for free. Not required for this update — just don't claim "all platforms" in absolute terms anywhere; keep language to "all major platforms" or name them explicitly, so it stays accurate as-is.
